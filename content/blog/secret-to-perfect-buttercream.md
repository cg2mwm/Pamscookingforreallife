## Why Most Buttercreams Fail

Grainy. Greasy. Too sweet. If you've ever made buttercream that ended up tasting like sugar and nothing else, you're not alone. After years of trial, I've narrowed it down to **three root causes**.

### Mistake #1: Cold Butter

Butter straight from the fridge won't emulsify properly. You want it at **65–68°F** — cool to touch but yields when pressed. Too warm and it goes greasy. Too cold and it won't cream.

**Fix:** Take butter out 45–60 minutes before baking. In winter, cut it into cubes to speed this up.

### Mistake #2: Skipping the Whipping Time

Most recipes say "beat until fluffy." Most people stop too early. Proper buttercream needs **5–7 full minutes** of high-speed beating after all the sugar is incorporated.

This whipping incorporates air and transforms the texture from pasty to silky.

### Mistake #3: Not Adding Enough Salt

Yes, even in sweet frosting. A pinch of fine sea salt doesn't make it salty — it makes it taste *more like itself*. It balances sweetness and rounds out the flavor.

**Fix:** Add 1/4 tsp fine sea salt per cup of butter used. Taste as you go.

## My Base Recipe

- 1 cup (225g) unsalted butter, room temperature
- 3 cups (360g) powdered sugar, sifted
- 2 tbsp heavy cream
- 1 tsp pure vanilla extract
- 1/4 tsp fine sea salt

Beat butter alone for 2 minutes. Add sugar in thirds. Add cream, vanilla, and salt. Beat 5–7 minutes until light and airy. Done.
